The History of Acrylic Paint Pouring

Acrylic paint pouring as we know it today is the result of centuries of artistic exploration and scientific discovery. Below is a timeline of several key events that have made acrylic pour art a revolutionary painting method.



15th Century

Painting with oils became a common artistic practice. However, due to the heightened skill needed to best work with the oil paint, along with the harsh chemicals that served as additives within the paints, and the pungent cleaning solutions, artists desired an alternative artform.

1934

The creation of acrylic resin and the adaptation of this substance into acrylic paint initiated the popularization of acrylic paint as a preferred substitute for oil based paint.

The Late 1930s

Mexican artist and political activist David Alfaro Siqueiros discovered the beauty of pour painting by accident. After examination of his unintentional wooden board masterpiece, he further studied the science behind the artistic medium and developed pour art. He loved the simplicity of the pour art process combined with the elaborate, stunning results.

The 1980s-2000s

As more attention was diverted toward scientific inquiry, studies into the viscocity of paint deepened. By recording the pouring of various paint colors on top of one another over a transparent surface, scientists found that the densities of the paints were responsible for different "floating" or "sinking" reactions, coined the "Rayleigh-Taylor instability".

2010s and Beyond

As the pour painting movement gained traction, people became fascinated with the artistic process and visually distinct outcomes of pour art. Acrylic pour painting is now a trending artform with artists all over the world creating and sharing both their superb works and insightful advice with others.
